<Section title="Background">
  Volpara Analytics existed to help mammography technologists improve their technique. Better technique means fewer missed cancers. The problem was nobody was using it.

  Slow dashboards, inconsistent metrics, no clear signal for when something needed attention. Technologists logged in so infrequently they had to relearn it every visit. Data that could improve patient outcomes was sitting unused.
</Section>

<Section label="Research" title="The data existed, the context didn't">
  Technologists had minutes between patients and no way to know if a score was good, bad, or worth acting on. We used Volpara's dataset to establish 160 unique thresholds, giving every metric a frame of reference for the first time.

<TwoCol>
  <Section slot="left" label="Design" title="The most important decision was to remove red">
    A co-design sprint with clinicians and technologists produced three principles: Actionable. Positive. Transparent.

We built a semantic colour system to better communicate our new thresholds. The first thing we cut was red. Underperformance became a focus area, not a failure. In a clinical environment, shame doesn’t change behaviour but clarity and positivity does.
  </Section>
