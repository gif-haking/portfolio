<Intro heading="Overview">
  Volpara's analytics platform helps radiographers understand the quality of the mammograms they take. The data existed — but nobody was looking at it. We redesigned the experience to make it actually useful.
</Intro>

<Section label="Research" title="Understanding the problem">
  We ran 14 moderated usability sessions across three sites in the US, UK, and Australia. The consistent finding: technologists trusted their instincts over the data because the dashboard felt like it was built for administrators, not for them.

  - Data was presented as raw numbers with no context
  - No indication of what was good or bad
  - No sense of personal progress over time
</Section>

<ImageFull src="/images/volpara/research.png" alt="Research synthesis board" caption="Affinity mapping from 14 user interviews" />

<Section label="Process" title="Finding the right frame">
  We explored three directions before landing on a fitness-tracker metaphor — personal, progressive, and motivating. Weekly scores, streaks, and peer benchmarks gave technologists something to care about.
</Section>

<ImageTwo caption="Early explorations vs final direction">

<Section label="Solution" title="Data that earns attention">
  The redesigned dashboard leads with a personal score, contextualised against site averages and personal history. Drill-down views let technologists understand specific metrics without overwhelming them upfront.
</Section>
